  • Green area cannot be developed
  • Reduces effective buildable land
  • Different rules for urban vs rural areas
  • Must comply with local planning regulations

Capital Gains Tax (CGT) - Property Sale Tax
Keywords: capital gains, CGT, capital gain tax, seller tax, profit tax, property sale tax, 20% tax

Seller pays either Capital Gains TAX or VAT

Capital Gains Tax

is paid if there is a profit made by the seller. (20% on the Gains/Profit). Capital gains tax is levied at a fixed rate of 20% on both individuals and companies. Capital gain tax is calculated based on gains arising from the disposal of immovable property situated in Cyprus including gains from the disposal of shares in companies, which own such immovable property.

The capital gain tax is calculated after deducting from the sales proceeds the original cost and any additional expenditure, adjusted for inflation, and any allowable expenses directly related to the disposal of the building such as interest on loan, real estate fees and advertising. It must be noted that if the property is purchased prior to 1980, the cost used in the calculation is the market value of the asset as at 1.1.1980.

However, there are some tax exemptions.

Who Pays: Buyer

VAT Rates Summary

Standard VAT: 19%

Reduced VAT: 5% (for first-time permanent residence with conditions)

Zero VAT: 0% (agricultural land, certain cases)

Reduced VAT Policy for Housing (5%)

New Policy (building permits after 31 Oct 2023):

  • 5% VAT on: First 130 sq.m of permanent residence
  • Max value eligible: €350,000
  • Total property value max: €475,000
  • Total buildable area max: 190 sq.m

Previous Policy (permits before 31 Oct 2023):

  • 5% VAT on: First 200 sq.m of residence
  • No price limit
  • Extra for large families: +15 sq.m per child (families with 3+ children)

Requirements for Reduced VAT

  • Commitment: Live in property for at least 10 years
  • Restriction: Cannot own another permanent residence in Cyprus
  • Penalty for early sale/rent: Must repay VAT difference (19% - 5%) prorated for remaining years

VAT on Land

Land Type VAT Rate

Commercial land 19%

Residential land (private seller, first-time or no sale in 10+ years) 0%

  • Applies to: Offices, retail, warehouses, hotels

Permanent Residence (PR) Programs
Keywords: PR, permanent residence, permanent residency, residence permit, fast track, immigration

Two PR Programs Available

Option 1: Regulation 6.2 (Fast Track via investment)

Option 2: Category F (Slow Track)

Fast Track PR - Regulation 6.2

Minimum Investment: €300,000

Investment Categories

  • Real Estate (Most Common): New house/apartment, €300,000+ (plus VAT), first-time sale from developer. Can be up to 2 housing units.
  • Commercial Real Estate: Offices, shops, hotels (new or resale), €300,000+
  • Company Shares: Cyprus company with physical presence and 5+ employees, €300,000+
  • Investment Funds: Cyprus AIF, AIFLNP, or RAIF units, €300,000+

Family Members Included

  • Main applicant + applicant's parents (NOT in-laws)
  • Spouse
  • Children up to age 25 (must be full-time students for Bachelor/Master degree)

Additional Requirements

  • Secured annual income from abroad: €50,000 + €15,000 for spouse + €10,000 per child
  • Example: Applicant + spouse + 2 children = €85,000 annual income required
  • Cannot work in Cyprus (except as shareholder/non-salaried director)
  • Clean criminal record for applicant and dependents 18+
  • Health insurance covering inpatient and outpatient care
  • Proof of residence: Title deed, sales agreement, or rental agreement

Adult Children Over 25

  • Investment multiplier: €300,000 × number of adult children
  • 1 adult child: €600,000 investment
  • 2 adult children: €900,000 investment
  • Income requirement per adult child: €50,000 + €15,000 for their spouse + €10,000 per dependent child

Processing Time

  • Timeframe: 6-10 months
  • Process: Civil Registry and Migration Department → Ministry of Interior

Required Documents

  • Passport copies (all applicants)
  • Criminal record certificates (certified, translated, Apostille) for 18+
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable, certified, translated, Apostille)
  • Proof of secured annual income (bank statements, tax returns, accountant certificate)
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) for main applicant and spouse
  • Student enrollment proof (for children 18-25)

Tax Residency in Cyprus
Keywords: tax residency, tax resident, 183 day rule, 60 day rule, residency test

Two Ways to Become Tax Resident

Rule 1: 183 Day Rule (Standard)

Rule 2: 60 Day Rule (Alternative)

183 Day Rule (Straightforward)

  • Requirement: Physical presence in Cyprus for more than 183 days in a tax year (1 Jan - 31 Dec)
  • Day counting: Both arrival and departure days count as presence days
  • Temporary absences: Do not break continuity of stay
  • Result: Become Cyprus tax resident

60 Day Rule (Flexible, More Complex)

ALL conditions must be met:

  • Condition 1: Stay in Cyprus for at least 60 days in the year
  • Condition 2: Carry on business in Cyprus (employed, self-employed, or director)
  • Condition 3: Maintain permanent home in Cyprus (owned or leased)
  • Condition 4: Do not reside in any other country for more than 183 days
  • Condition 5: Not a tax resident in any other country

Important: If business/employment stops during the year, person is NOT considered tax resident for that year.

Implications of Tax Residency

  • Taxable on worldwide income
  • Subject to special defense contribution on certain income (unless non-dom)
  • Eligible for Cyprus tax benefits and double taxation treaties

Non-Domicile (Non-Dom) Status
Keywords: non-dom, non domicile, tax exemption, dividend tax, interest tax, SDC exemption

Non-Dom Tax Benefits

Key Benefit: Exemption from Special Defense Contribution (SDC) on dividends and interest for 17 years

Who Qualifies for Non-Dom Status?

  • Criterion 1: Does NOT have domicile of origin in Cyprus
  • Criterion 2: Has obtained and maintained domicile of choice outside Cyprus
  • Criterion 3: Was NOT a Cyprus tax resident for 20 consecutive years before 16 July 2015

Tax Advantages

  • Dividends: 0% tax (exemption from SDC for 17 years)
  • Interest: 0% tax (exemption from SDC for 17 years)
  • Only contribution: GESY 2.65% on first €180,000 of relevant income

What is Special Defense Contribution (SDC)?

  • Standard rate on dividends: 17%
  • Standard rate on interest: 30%
  • Non-doms pay 0% for 17 years

Combined with Tax Residency

Non-dom status + Cyprus tax residency = extremely favorable tax regime for investors and high-net-worth individuals receiving passive income.

Company Share Transfer - Tax Advantages
Keywords: share transfer, company shares, tax optimization, no VAT, no transfer fees

Property Sale via Share Transfer

Method: Transfer shares of company that owns property (instead of transferring property directly)

Tax advantage: No VAT + No transfer fees

How It Works

  • Company owns the property
  • Sell shares of company (not the property itself)
  • Buyer becomes owner of company → indirectly owns property

Advantages

  • No VAT: Share sales are not subject to VAT
  • No transfer fees: Property doesn't change ownership (company does)
  • Simpler process: Often faster than property transfer
  • Cost savings: Especially significant for expensive properties

Important Consideration

  • Capital Gains Tax: Share sale subject to 20% CGT on profit
  • Due diligence essential: Buyer inherits ALL company liabilities (debts, lawsuits, etc.)
  • Legal structuring: Requires careful legal and tax planning
  • Company audit: Full financial audit recommended before purchase

When to Use This Method

  • High-value properties (where VAT/transfer fee savings are substantial)
  • Companies with single asset (property only)
  • Clean company structure (no hidden liabilities)
  • When minimizing transaction costs is priority

Example Savings (€1,000,000 Property)

  • Direct property purchase (new): €1,000,000 × 19% VAT = €190,000
  • Direct property purchase (resale): ~€49,375 transfer fees
  • Share transfer: €0 VAT + €0 transfer fees (only CGT on profit if any)

VAT Repayment on Early Sale/Rent
Keywords: VAT penalty, early sale, 10 year commitment, VAT repayment, reduced VAT violation

Penalty for Breaking 10-Year Commitment

Applies to: Properties purchased with reduced 5% VAT rate

Commitment: Must use as main residence for 10 years

Penalty: Repay VAT difference (14%) prorated for remaining years

When Penalty Applies

  • Sell property before 10 years
  • Rent property before 10 years
  • Stop using as main residence before 10 years

How Penalty is Calculated

Formula: VAT Difference × (Remaining Years / 10)

  • VAT Difference: 19% - 5% = 14%
  • Prorated: Pay only for remaining years of 10-year period

Example Calculation

Property purchased for €600,000:

  • VAT paid at 5%: €30,000
  • Standard VAT would be 19%: €114,000
  • VAT difference: €84,000
  • If sold after 3 years (7 years remaining):
  • Penalty: €84,000 × (7/10) = €58,800
  • If sold after 7 years (3 years remaining):
  • Penalty: €84,000 × (3/10) = €25,200

Procedure

  • Must notify Registrar within 30 days of termination of use
  • Pay VAT difference for remaining period
  • Cannot transfer property until payment made

Exceptions (No Penalty)

  • Death of owner: No penalty if owner dies
  • Transfer to adult child: No penalty if property transferred to adult child of owner

VAT Reclaim on Residential Land
Keywords: land VAT, VAT reclaim, VAT refund, residential land, 14% refund, building on land

14% VAT Refund When Building Own Permanent Residence

Special Case: Individual buying residential land (<1,500m²) to build own permanent residence

Refund: Can claim back 14% VAT after building and living for 6 months

How It Works

  • Step 1: Buy residential land and pay 19% VAT upfront
  • Step 2: Build your permanent residence on the land
  • Step 3: Live in residence for at least 6 months
  • Step 4: Apply for 14% VAT refund
  • Result: Effective VAT rate becomes 5% (19% - 14% = 5%)

Eligibility Requirements

  • Land must be residential zoning
  • Land size under 1,500 m²
  • Buyer must be individual (not company)
  • Must be for own permanent residence
  • Must actually build and occupy for 6 months before claiming

Example Calculation

  • Land price: €200,000
  • VAT paid at purchase: €200,000 × 19% = €38,000
  • After building and living 6 months, claim: €200,000 × 14% = €28,000 refund
  • Net VAT paid: €38,000 - €28,000 = €10,000 (5% effective rate)
