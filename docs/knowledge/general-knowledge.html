AML/KYC Record Keeping
Procedure for New Cases

Subject: AML/KYC Record Keeping – Procedure for New Cases

From now on, for every new case, please make sure to request from the lawyer handling the transaction a PDF file containing the key documents of the case. This file should include at least the Sales Agreement.

This document should be sent to us as a copy, as we are directly involved in the transaction and, according to Law 188(I)/2007, we are also legally required to maintain AML/KYC records for our clients — just like the lawyers are.

2 / 36
Legal Obligation

Our Legal Requirement

According to Law 188(I)/2007, we are legally required to maintain AML/KYC records for our clients.

This is not just a preference or internal policy - it's a legal obligation that applies to all parties involved in real estate transactions.

Key Points:

  • We are directly involved in the transaction
  • The law applies to us just as it applies to lawyers
  • Failure to comply can result in legal consequences
  • Documentation must be maintained for the legally required period

10 / 36
Green Area Requirements
Local Town Plans / City's Regional areas

Green Area Deduction Based on Field Size

Field Size Green Area Deduction
Up to 800 sq.m. 0% Green area
800 sq.m. – 1,500 sq.m. 5% Green area
1,500 sq.m. – 2,500 sq.m. 10% Green area
2,500 sq.m. and above 15% Green area

These percentages represent the portion of the field that must be designated as green area and cannot be developed.

11 / 36
Green Area Requirements
Rural Town Plan (ΔΗΛΩΣΗ ΠΟΛΙΤΙΚΗΣ)

Rural Area Regulations

For areas in Rural Town Plan, the maximum area for public green deducted is 10%

Key Points:

  • Parcels with area less than 2,500 sq.m. may not have public green deducted
  • Parcels with area 2,500 sq.m. or over: green area deduction will be decreasing so the final area is not less than 2,000 sq.m.

Why Rural Requirements are Different:

  • Rural areas already have a lot of green spaces
  • Development considerations differ from urban areas
  • Balance between development and preservation

Permanent Residence Permit (PR)

Two Different Programs

Available Options:

  • Regulation 6.2 (Fast Track – via investment)
  • Category F (so-called "Slow Track" program)

Key Differences:

  • Fast Track: Based on investment, quicker processing
  • Slow Track: Based on other criteria, longer processing time

Why Choose PR in Cyprus:

  • Right to live in Cyprus indefinitely
  • Access to EU benefits
  • Path to citizenship after certain period
  • Family reunification options
17 / 36
PR – Fast Track under Regulation 6(2)
Family Members

Who Can Be Included?

Eligible Family Members:

  • The main applicant (and their parents – not in-laws)
  • The spouse
  • The children up to the age of 25 (studying for Bachelor or Master's degree)

Important Notes:

  • Adult children over 25 are not eligible under standard program
  • In-laws (parents of spouse) are not included
  • Children must be full-time students to qualify after age 18
  • Documentation required to prove family relationships
18 / 36
Investment Categories for PR

Minimum Investment: EUR 300,000

Option 1: Real Estate

Investment in a new house/apartment: Up to two housing units, minimum total market value of EUR 300,000 (plus VAT). Must be first-time sale from land development company.

Other Investment Options:

  • Commercial Real Estate: Offices, shops, hotels or similar developments (new or resale)
  • Company Shares: Investment in share capital of Cyprus company with proven physical presence and at least 5 employees
  • Investment Funds: Units in Cyprus Collective Investment Organization (AIF, AIFLNP, RAIF)

Key Points:

  • Properties don't need to be purchased from same development company
  • Investment can be made by entity where applicant is sole shareholder

Qualitative Criteria for PR

Additional Requirements

Income Requirements:

  • Secured annual income from abroad: EUR 50,000
  • Additional EUR 15,000 for spouse
  • Additional EUR 10,000 for each dependent child

Example: Applicant + spouse + 2 children = EUR 85,000

Other Criteria:

  • Declaration that applicant and dependents shall not work or engage in business in Cyprus (except as shareholders/non-salaried directors)
  • Clear criminal record certificate for applicant and dependents over 18
  • Health insurance policy covering inpatient and outpatient care
  • Proof of residence in Cyprus (Title Deed, Sales Agreement or Rental Agreement)
20 / 36
Higher Value Investment for Adult Children

Including Adult Children Over 25

Investment Multiplier:

The market value of EUR 300,000 investment should be multiplied by the number of adult children invoking the same investment.

  • 1 adult child: EUR 600,000 investment
  • 2 adult children: EUR 900,000 investment
  • And so on...

Additional Requirements:

  • Each adult child must prove secured annual income of at least EUR 50,000
  • Additional EUR 15,000 for spouse and EUR 10,000 for each dependent child
  • Proof of payment of at least 66% of market value upon application
  • Investment may be made jointly with adult child or solely in applicant's name

VAT Policy for Housing

The New VAT Policy

New Reduced VAT Policy:

  • Reduced VAT from 19% to 5% for first 130 sq.m on main residence
  • Up to value of €350,000
  • Total value not exceeding €475,000
  • Total buildable area not exceeding 190 sq.m

Previous VAT Policy (for permits before 31 Oct 2023):

  • 5% for first net 200 sq.m of residence
  • No price limit
  • For families with more than 3 children: additional 15 sq.m per child

Main Requirements:

  • Applicant must not have another permanent residence in Cyprus

Tax Residency - 183 Day Rule

Standard Tax Residency Test

Cyprus has adopted a residency-based system of taxation, whereby physical presence in Cyprus exceeding 183 days in a tax year (1st January to 31st December) will constitute tax residency for individuals.

Key Points:

  • Physical presence of more than 183 days in a calendar year
  • Days of arrival and departure are both counted as days of presence
  • Temporary absences do not break the continuity of stay
  • Most straightforward way to establish tax residency

Implications of Tax Residency:

  • Taxable on worldwide income
  • Subject to special defense contribution on certain income types
  • Eligible for tax benefits and treaties
25 / 36
Tax Residency - 60 Day Rule

Alternative Tax Residency Test

Conditions for 60-Day Rule:

  • Individual stays in Cyprus for at least 60 days in the year
  • Carries on a business in Cyprus (employed, self-employed, or director)
  • Maintains a permanent home in Cyprus (owned or leased)
  • Does not reside in any other country for more than 183 days
  • Is not a tax resident in any other country

Important Notes:

  • If business/employment stops during the year, person is not considered tax resident for that year
  • More complex than 183-day rule but allows for more flexibility
  • Requires careful documentation of all conditions

Non-Domicile Status

Exemption from Special Contribution for Defense

An individual is granted exemption from special contribution for defense on dividends and interest received either in Cyprus or abroad, provided that such individual is considered as non-domiciled of Cyprus.

Criteria for Non-Dom Status:

  • Does not have domicile of origin in Cyprus
  • Has obtained and maintained a domicile of choice outside Cyprus
  • Was not a Cyprus tax resident for 20 consecutive years prior to introduction of law (16 July 2015)

Benefits:

  • Exemption from tax on dividends and interest for 17 years
  • Only subject to GESY contribution (2.65% on first €180,000 income)
  • Significant tax planning advantage

Tax on Employment Income

Income Tax Rates

50% Exemption:

Individuals with annual employment income exceeding €55,000 who take up first employment in Cyprus may be eligible for an exemption from taxation of 50% of their employment income from sources in Cyprus.

Exemption Details:

  • Applies for 17 years from year of employment
  • Available to individuals not Cyprus tax residents for 15 consecutive years prior
  • Significant tax incentive for high-earners

Standard Income Tax Rates:

  • 0% up to €19,500
  • 20% on €19,501 - €28,000
  • 25% on €28,001 - €36,300
  • 30% on €36,301 - €60,000
  • 35% over €60,000

Social Insurance Contributions

Social Insurance System

Any person who is undertaking employment activities in Cyprus is subject to social insurance contributions in Cyprus.

Contribution Rates for 2024:

  • Employee contribution: 8.8%
  • Employer contribution: 8.8%
  • Total: 17.6%

Contribution Caps:

  • Monthly earnings cap: €5,239
  • Annual earnings cap: €62,868
  • Anything above these thresholds is not subject to social insurance contributions

Benefits of Social Insurance:

  • Access to public healthcare system (GESY)
  • Pension entitlements
  • Unemployment benefits
  • Maternity/paternity benefits

Buyer Taxes

Transfer Fees vs. VAT

Buyer pays either Transfer Fees or VAT, depending on the property type.

Transfer Fees:

  • Paid on Resale Properties
  • Paid on Plots/Land Parcels not subject to VAT
  • Calculated based on property value
  • 0% discount on first €85,000, 50% discount thereafter

VAT:

  • Paid on New Properties
  • Paid on Plots/Land Parcels subject to VAT
  • Standard rate: 19%
  • Reduced rate: 5% for first-time buyers meeting criteria

Unusual Cases:

In rare situations, both Transfer Fees and VAT may need to be paid when there's a significant difference between purchase price and land registry valuation.

30 / 36
Seller Taxes - Capital Gains

Capital Gains Tax

Capital Gains Tax is paid if there is a profit made by the seller (20% on the Gains/Profit).

Calculation:

  • Capital gain tax is levied at a fixed rate of 20%
  • Calculated on gains from disposal of immovable property in Cyprus
  • Also applies to disposal of shares in companies owning such property

Deductions:

  • Original cost of property
  • Additional expenditure (adjusted for inflation)
  • Allowable expenses directly related to disposal
  • Interest on loan, real estate fees, advertising costs

Special Rules:

  • If property purchased before 1980, market value as of 1.1.1980 is used
  • Certain exemptions may apply
31 / 36
Seller Taxes - VAT in Rare Cases

VAT Repayment on Early Sale

If an eligible person who used the reduced VAT policy does not use their house as main residence for at least 10 years and proceeds with a sale or rent prior to the lapse of 10 years, they must pay the difference between reduced VAT (5%) and standard VAT (19%).

Procedure:

  • Must notify Registrar within 30 days of termination of use
  • Pay difference for remaining period of 10-year commitment

Example Calculation:

  • House bought for €600,000 at 5% VAT = €30,000
  • Standard VAT would be €600,000 × 19% = €114,000
  • Difference = €84,000
  • If sold after 3 years: €84,000 × 7/10 = €58,800 repayment

Exceptions:

  • Death of owner
  • Transfer to adult child of owner

Refugee Compensation Fee

Additional Seller Fee

Sellers also pay a small fee for the compensation of refugees in Cyprus: 0.004% on the selling price.

Examples:

  • For €100,000 selling price: €400 fee
  • For €180,000 selling price: €720 fee
  • For €300,000 selling price: €1,200 fee

Purpose:

  • Contributes to refugee compensation fund
  • Standard fee on all property transactions
  • Applies regardless of seller nationality

Payment:

  • Paid by seller at time of transaction
  • Processed through land registry department
  • Required for transfer of ownership

VAT on Land

VAT Rates by Land Type

VAT Rates:

  • Agricultural land: 0% VAT
  • Commercial land: 19% VAT
  • Residential land (company seller): 19% VAT
  • Residential land (private individual): 0% or 19% VAT

Private Individual Selling Residential Land:

  • 0% if first-time seller or no sales in past 10+ years
  • 19% if previous property sold in last 10 years

Special Case - Building Permanent Residence:

  • Buyer individual purchasing residential land (<1,500m²) for own permanent residence
  • Even though 19% VAT paid upfront, may claim back 14% after building
  • Must use residence for 6 months before claiming

35 / 36
VAT on Commercial Properties

Standard VAT Rates

The standard VAT rate for commercial properties or properties used as a non-permanent residence is 19%.

Property Types Subject to 19% VAT:

  • Office buildings
  • Retail spaces
  • Warehouses
  • Hotels
  • Properties for non-permanent residence

Special Case - Short-Term Lettings:

  • Properties intended for short-term lettings (at least 10 years): 0% VAT
  • Rationality: VAT will be charged on daily basis like hotel rooms
  • Applies to properties operated as hospitality businesses
36 / 36
Special VAT Cases

Unique VAT Situations

Company Share Transfer:

If the sale involves the transfer/sale of shares of a company that owns the property, there is no VAT applicable (and no transfer fees either).

Advantages of Share Transfer:

  • No VAT or transfer fees
  • Simpler transaction process
  • Often used for expensive properties

Important Consideration:

  • Sale of shares is subject to capital gains tax at 20%
  • Requires careful legal and tax structuring
  • Due diligence on company liabilities is essential

When to Use This Approach:

  • High-value properties
  • Companies with single asset
  • When minimizing transaction costs is priority
